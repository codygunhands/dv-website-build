Bill of sale guide (template included)

What to include, when to use it, and a clean template for high-ticket items, equipment, and Marketplace deals.

A bill of sale is the receipt that proves a transaction actually happened. For higher-ticket private sales - vehicles, equipment, tools, electronics - it's the difference between "we both remember what happened" and "this is a clean record I can show my CPA, my insurance, or a court if it ever comes to that."

Note
If the item is worth more than a few hundred dollars or you're selling to a stranger, write one. It takes 60 seconds and it protects both sides.

What to include

  • Date of sale
  • Buyer + seller - full legal name, address, phone, email
  • Item description - make, model, year, serial/VIN, condition
  • Purchase price in numerals and words
  • Payment method - cash, check, ACH, Stripe, etc.
  • "Sold as-is" language - unless you're including a warranty
  • Signatures + dates from both parties

When to use it

  • Selling a vehicle, motorcycle, trailer, or boat
  • Private sale of equipment, tools, or appliances
  • Marketplace deals over $300
  • Any deal where the buyer might want documentation for insurance or registration

Bill of sale template (copy/paste)

BILL OF SALE

Date: _____________________________

SELLER
Name: _____________________________
Address: __________________________
Phone: ____________________________
Email: ____________________________

BUYER
Name: _____________________________
Address: __________________________
Phone: ____________________________
Email: ____________________________

ITEM SOLD
Description: ______________________
Make / Model: _____________________
Year: _____________________________
Serial / VIN: _____________________
Condition: ________________________

PURCHASE PRICE
Amount (numerals): $______________
Amount (words): ___________________
Payment method: ___________________

The seller hereby transfers ownership of the item described
above to the buyer on an "as-is, where-is" basis with no
warranty, expressed or implied, except as noted below:

Notes / warranty: ________________________________________

By signing below, both parties acknowledge the transaction
described above has occurred.

Seller signature: ______________  Date: __________
Buyer signature: _______________  Date: __________

Pro tips

  • Keep a digital copy. Take a clear photo of the signed bill of sale before the buyer leaves.
  • If you accept cash, include a line that says "cash received in full at signing."
  • For vehicles, check your state's DMV - many require a specific bill-of-sale form for title transfer.
  • If the buyer asks you to sign before they pay, don't.
