Facebook Marketplace bill of sale (free) + safest way to get paid

Bill of sale template for Marketplace, how to sell safely in person, safest payment (cash, Zelle, ACH), scam red flags, and a clean handover checklist for tools, vehicles, and higher-ticket items.

Facebook Marketplace is the busiest classifieds site in America. It is also where most private-sale scams happen. If you want to sell safely on Facebook Marketplace, you need a short playbook before you list, the safest way to get paid picked in advance, and a one-page bill of sale for Facebook Marketplace ready to sign at the handover.

Note
90% of safe Marketplace sales come down to four things: clear terms in your listing, payment confirmed before handover, a signed bill of sale, and a documented "done" moment. Everything else is optional.

Facebook Marketplace bill of sale (free template)

For anything over a couple hundred dollars - tools, electronics, furniture, vehicles, trailers, or anything titled - sign a bill of sale at the handover. It protects both sides if there's ever a dispute, and it's required for the buyer to register a vehicle or trailer in most states.

Copy this into your phone's Notes app, fill in the blanks, both parties sign, take a photo. That's it.

BILL OF SALE

Date: __________

Seller: ____________________________________
Address: ___________________________________
Phone: _____________________________________

Buyer: _____________________________________
Address: ___________________________________
Phone: _____________________________________

Item sold: _________________________________
Description (make/model/serial/VIN/condition):
___________________________________________
___________________________________________

Sale price: $______________  Payment method: __________
("Cash"  /  "Zelle ref ________"  /  "ACH ref ________")

The seller confirms payment was received in full on the date
above. The item is sold AS-IS, with no warranty expressed or
implied. The buyer accepts the item in its current condition.

Seller signature: __________________________ Date: ______
Buyer signature:  __________________________ Date: ______

Need a longer version (with odometer disclosure, lien-release language, or notary blocks)? See the full bill of sale guide - same structure, more fields for vehicles and equipment.

Before you list

  • Take real photos in good light. Show defects honestly.
  • Write a 3-sentence description: what it is, condition, and what's included.
  • Set a fair, firm price. "OBO" invites lowball offers.
  • Add your terms: cash or Zelle/ACH only, in-person at a public location, no shipping.
  • Mention you'll sign a bill of sale at handover - it filters out scammers and reassures real buyers.

Talking to buyers

  • Don't share your phone number until they've committed to a time.
  • Beware of buyers who ask to "pay extra to ship" or want to use weird third-party escrow links.
  • If they ask to send a code to verify you're real - that's a Google Voice scam. Block them.
  • Real buyers ask normal questions: "Is it still available?", "Can I see it tomorrow at 10?"

The safest way to get paid on Facebook Marketplace

Three payment methods are safe for in-person Marketplace sales. Everything else is a fraud vector.

  • Cash - best for anything under ~$500. Count it in front of the buyer. For larger amounts, meet at your bank and deposit it before handover.
  • Zelle - instant, free, no chargebacks. Confirm the money is in your account before you hand over the item, not just a "sent" notification on the buyer's phone (those are fakeable).
  • ACH bank transfer - fine if you can wait 1–3 business days for it to clear. Don't release the item until your bank shows the deposit as Available, not just Pending.
Warning
Never accept Venmo, Cash App, or PayPal Goods & Services for in-person sales - buyers can dispute the charge weeks later under "item not as described" and get the money back. PayPal Friends & Family and Venmo can be reversed too if the buyer claims account fraud. Stick to cash or Zelle for sales over $100.

The handover

  • Meet during daylight at a public spot - many police stations have designated exchange zones.
  • Bring a friend if it's a higher-ticket item or you've got a weird feeling.
  • Confirm the payment cleared. Cash: count it. Zelle/ACH: see it in your account.
  • Hand over the item only after payment is confirmed.
  • Sign the bill of sale on your phone (template above). Take a photo of the signed copy before the buyer leaves.

The "done" moment

This is the one most people skip. After the deal:

  • Take a photo of the signed bill of sale.
  • Mark the listing as Sold.
  • Save the buyer's name, phone, and the bill of sale in one place.
  • If it was a titled item (vehicle, trailer), file with the DMV that day.

Red flags - block immediately

  • "I'll send my driver to pick it up"
  • "My check will be for more than the price - can you refund the difference?"
  • "Send me your bank info / SSN / DL upfront"
  • "Click this link to confirm shipping"
  • "I'm out of town, let me PayPal you"
  • "Send me a Google Voice code to confirm you're real"

FAQ

Do you need a bill of sale for Facebook Marketplace?

Not legally required for most items, but strongly recommended for anything over a couple hundred dollars. It's mandatory in most states for vehicles, trailers, and titled equipment so the buyer can register the title. A signed bill of sale also protects you if the buyer claims the item was misrepresented.

What is the safest way to pay on Facebook Marketplace?

Cash for anything under $500, Zelle for larger amounts (instant, no chargebacks). Avoid Venmo, Cash App, and PayPal for in-person sales - they can be reversed weeks later.

Can a Facebook Marketplace bill of sale be handwritten?

Yes. A bill of sale just needs the date, names and addresses of both parties, the item description, the price and payment method, and both signatures. Handwritten on paper or typed in your phone's Notes app - both are valid in all 50 states. Take a photo of the signed copy before the buyer leaves.

How do I avoid scams on Facebook Marketplace?

Sell in-person only, take cash or Zelle only, never share your bank info or SSN, never accept overpayment "by mistake," and never click a payment-confirmation link sent in chat. If a buyer pushes any of those, block and move on - there are always more buyers.
