What it does

Built around the problem, not around a roadmap.

The product solves something specific and gets out of your way.

  • 01

    Ohm's Law - 1Φ + 3Φ

    Solve for the missing variable across V, I, and R; power is shown alongside. Toggle 3Φ in the app bar to use line-to-line voltage and power factor for kW. Quick reference any time you're staring at a scope or a meter.

  • 02

    Power / kW / kVA

    Apparent (kVA) and real (kW) power from amps, volts, and power factor. Convert between amps, kW, and kVA on single or three-phase. Useful for sizing generators and transformers in the field.

  • 03

    Wire sizing - NEC 310.15 derating

    Pick load (amps, kW, or kVA), conductor (Cu / Al), continuous vs non-continuous (125% applied), then dial in ambient temperature, current-carrying conductors, and 60/75/90 °C terminal rating. Returns minimum AWG / kcmil with the derated ampacity called out.

  • 04

    Voltage drop - CMA + NEC Ch. 9 Table 9

    Two methods, your choice. Quick CMA (K · I · L) for the 3% rule of thumb, or NEC Ch. 9 Table 9 with effective Z (PVC raceway, pf 0.85) when you need the conservative Code answer. Up-size before pulling a 200-foot run.

  • 05

    Conduit fill - mixed conductors (Pro)

    EMT, PVC Sch 40 / Sch 80, RMC, IMC, ENT. NEC Chapter 9, Table 1 fill rules (53% / 31% / 40%). Mix conductor types and insulations in the same raceway and get the minimum trade size.

  • 06

    Motor FLA + OCPD (Pro)

    NEC Tables 430.250 / 430.248 full-load amps, 430.52 sizing for inverse-time breakers and dual-element fuses (175% / 300% / 250%), 430.22 conductor sizing at 125% FLA. Single and three-phase.

  • 07

    Transformer sizing (Pro)

    kVA → primary and secondary FLA, 450.3(B) primary OCPD, conductor sizing recommendations. Single and three-phase, 240 V / 480 V / 208 V common configurations.

  • 08

    Box fill - NEC 314.16 (Pro)

    Conductors, devices, equipment grounding conductors, and internal cubic-inch volume. Returns required box volume so you don't get red-tagged for cramming a 4" sq with too many wires.

  • 09

    Service load - NEC 220.82 + 220.83 (Pro)

    Both methods. 220.82 standard (new dwelling, first 10 kVA at 100% / remainder at 40% + 220.82(C) HVAC). 220.83 optional (existing dwelling adding loads, first 8 kVA at 100% / remainder at 40%). Right-size the panel.

  • 10

    Grounding - EGC + GEC (Pro)

    Equipment grounding conductor (NEC 250.122) by OCPD rating. Grounding electrode conductor (NEC 250.66) by service-entrance conductor. Cu / Al for both.

  • 11

    GFCI / AFCI required-locations (Pro)

    Quick reference for NEC 210.8 (GFCI) and 210.12 (AFCI) - bathrooms, kitchens, garages, outdoor, basements, dwelling-unit branch circuits. Stop digging through the codebook on the truck.

  • 12
